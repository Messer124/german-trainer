wenn

The conjunction wenn can have two meanings: if and when:

Wenn Sie nichts dagegen haben, mache ich das Fenster auf. — If you don't mind, I will open the window.

Wenn es regnet, sitze ich zu Hause. — When it rains, I stay at home.

als

The conjunction als also means when, but it is used only for a single completed action in the past:

Compare:

Als ich nach Deutschland kam, war ich sehr glücklich. — When I arrived in Germany, I was very happy. That means I arrived in Germany once in the past and was very happy then.

Wenn ich nach Deutschland kam, war ich sehr glücklich. — When I used to come to Germany, I was very happy. That is: every time, repeatedly, when I came to Germany, I was happy.

A helpful trick is to mentally insert always or every time before when. If that works, choose wenn.

So, wenn is used for repeated actions in the past, and also in the present and future; als is used for a single action in the past.
