Usage and translation details:

weil

Weil is synonymous with the coordinating conjunction denn, but it forms a subordinate clause.

Compare:

Ich gehe heute ins Kino, denn ich mag den Film. — I am going to the cinema today because I like the movie.

Ich gehe heute ins Kino, weil ich den Film mag. — I am going to the cinema today because I like the movie.

dass

Translated as "that"

Ich weiß, dass Berlin schön ist. — I know that Berlin is beautiful.

Ich glaube, dass er etwas faul ist. — I think that he is a little lazy.

damit

It is translated as "for this purpose" or simply "to". The mandatory condition is different subjects in the main clause and the subordinate clause.

Compare:

1. Ich verdiene Geld, um gut zu leben. — I earn money to live well (same subject). Here we use the infinitive construction um ... zu

2. Ich verdiene Geld, damit mein Sohn gut lebt. — I earn money so that my son lives well. I earn / my son lives — two different subjects, so we use the subordinating conjunction damit.
