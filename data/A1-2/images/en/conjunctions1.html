Coordinating conjunctions:
aber (but), und (and), denn (because), oder (or), sondern (but rather). They connect words in a sentence, phrases, or independent clauses. The key feature of coordinating conjunctions is that they do not affect word order in the sentence.

Conjunctive adverbs. The most common are:
deswegen (therefore), dann (then), jedoch (however), sonst (otherwise), trotzdem (nevertheless).

Conjunctive adverbs also connect simple clauses into compound sentences, but differ from coordinating conjunctions in that:

  1. a comma is always placed before them;
  2. they always refer back to what was said in the first part of the compound sentence;
  3. they take the first position in their clause, so the predicate (or its finite part) comes right after them.

Compare:

Ich gehe heute ins Kino, denn ich mag den Film. — I am going to the cinema today because I like this movie.

Ich mag den Film, deswegen gehe ich heute ins Kino. — I like this movie, therefore I am going to the cinema today.
