Besides compound sentences, German also has complex sentences, which combine a main clause and a subordinate clause.

They are formed with subordinating conjunctions.
The most common are weil (because), dass (that), wenn (when / if), als (when), damit (so that).

Important to remember:

1. In a subordinate clause, the finite verb is always in the final position:

Du musst dich beeilen, weil der Film bald anfängt. — You need to hurry, because the movie starts soon.

2. If there are several verbs in the subordinate clause, the finite verb is placed at the very end.

Ich glaube, dass man Rauchen verbieten muss. — I think that smoking should be banned.

3. A subordinate clause can stand before the main clause. In that case, it takes the first position, so the verb in the main clause comes immediately after it.

Wenn er wieder kommt, besprechen wir das. — When he comes again, we will discuss it.
