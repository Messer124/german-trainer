The passive voice (Passiv) describes an action directed at a person or object and performed by someone else:

  • The car is being repaired now (someone is repairing the car now).
  • The house is being built (someone is building the house now).
  • Why isn't this room being cleaned (why isn't someone cleaning this room?).

In other words, the noun (or the pronoun replacing it) is the subject of the sentence, but it does not perform the action itself: the action is performed on it by someone else.

An action can:

Be carried out in the present

The house is being built.

Already be completed by the present moment

The house is built. (Finally!)

Be carried out in the future

The house will be built.

Be completed in the future

The house will be completed. (We need to wait.)

Be carried out in the past

The house was being built.

Be completed in the past

The house had been built.

So, Passiv is used when we need to emphasize the action itself rather than who performs it. Compare:

Der Lehrer erklärt uns das Passiv. — The teacher explains the passive voice to us (the “hero” of the statement is the teacher).

Uns wird das Passiv erklärt. — The passive voice is explained to us (the “hero” of the statement is the passive voice).
