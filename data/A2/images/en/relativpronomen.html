Relative pronouns (der, die, das)

Relativpronomen are relative pronouns: special article-like words used without a noun, placed at the beginning of a subordinate clause and declined by case.

They are needed to build sentences containing words like "who/which/that". In most cases they match the definite article.

How to use a relative pronoun:

Example: "This is the man whom I know" â€” Das ist der Mann, den ich kenne.

  1. Identify the noun gender that will be replaced
    man → masculine (Maskulin)
  2. Identify the case by asking the verb in the clause
    I know (whom? what?) → man (Akkusativ)
  3. Choose the matching definite article for that gender and case
    Maskulin, Akkusativ → den
  4. If the case is not Genitiv and not plural Dativ, the relative pronoun usually matches the definite article
    Den (definite article) = den (Relativpronomen)
