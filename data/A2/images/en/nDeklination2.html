Nouns that belong to weak declension

1st
group:		 Nouns ending in “-e” that refer to living beings (people/animals): der Kunde (customer), der Alte (old man), der Löwe (lion). They take the ending -n in Akk, Dat, Gen.
2nd
group:		 masculine nationalities ending in “-e”: der Russe, der Türke, der Franzose They take the ending -n in Akk, Dat, Gen.
3rd
group:		 Abstract masculine nouns ending in “-e”: der Name (name), der Buchstabe (letter), der Gedanke (thought). They take the ending -n in Akk, Dat and the ending -ns in Gen.
4th
group:		 monosyllabic nouns referring to people or animals: der Mensch (person), der Bär (bear), der Herr (gentleman), der Graf (count), der Prinz (prince), der Held (hero). They take the ending -en in Akk, Dat, Gen.
5th
group:		 Loanwords with the suffixes “-and-”, “-ant-”, “-ent-”, “-et-”, “-ot-”, “-oge-”, “-at-”, “-ist-”, “-nom-”, “-soph-”, “-graf-”, “-loge-”, when these syllables are stressed: der Diplomat, der Patriot, der Polizist, der Agronom, der Philosoph. They take the ending -en in Akk, Dat, Gen.
