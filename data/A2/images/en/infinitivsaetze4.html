An infinitive construction can also stand before the main clause. In that case, the conjugated part of the verb in the main clause takes first position:

Anstatt Klavier zu spielen, hat er den ganzen Abend Fußball gespielt. — Instead of playing the piano, he played football all evening.

It is important not to confuse infinitive constructions with ordinary subordinate clauses, because unlike infinitive clauses, subordinate clauses must contain a subject.

Infinitive construction Subordinate clause
Ich lerne fleißig, um viel zu wissen. — I study hard in order to know a lot. Ich lerne fleißig, damit ich viel weiß. — I study hard so that I know a lot.
Paul geht lieber ins Kino, anstatt zu lernen. — Paul would rather go to the cinema instead of studying. Paul geht lieber ins Kino, anstatt dass er lernt. — Paul goes to the cinema instead of studying.
Er geht, ohne Tschüss zu sagen. — He leaves without saying goodbye. Er geht, ohne dass er Tschüss sagt. — He leaves without saying goodbye.
