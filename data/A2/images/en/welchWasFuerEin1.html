In German there are two kinds of questions that can both be translated as: what kind of? which?

These are the questions:

Was für ein/eine ... ? — Was für einen Mantel suchst du? — What kind of coat are you looking for?

Welcher/welche/welches ... ? — Welchen Mantel suchst du? — Which coat are you looking for?

So what is the difference?

Was für ein ... ?
“What kind of” in the sense of “one of a kind/category”

Was für einen Mantel suchst du? — Einen eleganten.
What kind of coat are you looking for? — An elegant one.

Welch- ?
“Which” in the sense of “which exact one”

Welchen Mantel suchst du: den schwarzen oder den grauen? — Which coat are you looking for: the black one or the grey one?

Remember:

The answer to the question «Was für ein ... ?» contains an indefinite article in the singular. In the plural there is no article, just as in the question itself: Was für Schuhe? What kind of shoes?

The answer to the question «Welch- ?» contains the definite article.
