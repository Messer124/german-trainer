The infinitive is the basic form of a verb. A verb in the infinitive answers the question “what to do?”. In German, infinitive verbs usually end in -en or -n: lernen, machen, tun.

A subordinate clause containing the particle zu + a verb in the infinitive is called an infinitive clause.

Example:

Ich hoffe, die Prüfung zu bestehen. — I hope to pass the exam.

The particle zu stands:

with separable-prefix verbs, between the prefix and the stem:

Er versucht, das Fenster aufzumachen. — He is trying to open the window.

Ich habe keine Zeit, mein Zimmer ständig aufzuräumen. — I have no time to keep tidying my room.

in all other cases, directly before the verb:

Ich habe Lust, ins Kino zu gehen. — I feel like going to the cinema.

Sie versucht, dich zu verstehen. — She is trying to understand you.
